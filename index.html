Embrace a Sustainable Future

Sustainable Green Campus

Half of the seven-acre campus is covered by water bodies, greenery, and a cleansing biotope, making it a lush and eco-friendly environment.

Innovative Architecture

The 13-storey building utilises wind, water, and light to create a tropical microclimate, promoting environmental and social sustainability.

Breathing Building

The structure features breezeways that offer cross-ventilation, allowing for hybrid ventilation systems that utilise fans and air conditioners.

Sun-drenched Campus

The design of the building largely utilises natural sunlight, thereby reducing the need for artificial lighting.

Draped in Green

Lush greenery, both inside and outside, provides shade, temperature control, improved air quality, and aesthetics.

Room and Floor Identification

It’s an easy sequence and you’ll get the hang of it in no time.

Example: 07A-01C

  • - The first two digits, 07, refer to the floor number.
  • - The next alphabet, A, refers to the block on the floor. Each floor is divided into 8 blocks.
  • - The next two digits, 01, refer to the room number.
  • - The last alphabet, C, refers to the use of the room. C means classroom, T means lecture theatre and L means lab.

Universal Accessibility

The campus has ample access options for the differently abled, including ramps and lifts on every floor, and specially-fitted toilets and parking spots.

Guardians of the Campus

Access Points

There are four access points around the campus incorporating universal accessibility for your convenience.

Use Of Stairs & Escalators

Lifts are primarily meant for the differently abled. The campus encourages skipping lift queues for wellness-inducing walks with timesaving stairs and escalators. Each step on the escalators is meant for one person.

Hybrid Ventilation System

The building’s hybrid ventilation system automatically adopts comfortable temperatures, so please do not adjust regulators and windows in the classrooms manually.
